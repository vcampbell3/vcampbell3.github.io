## Non-negotiables / table stakes

These are the things that seem clearly established:

1. **I do not want a low-sitting vehicle again.**
   - My 2017 Camry feels too low and I dislike getting in and out of it.
   - I want a higher seating position and a more commanding view of the road.

2. **Ride / quiet / comfort matter a lot.**
   - I want something that feels relaxed, smooth, substantial, and good for road trips.

3. **I want at least decent power, and likely more than that.**
   - I do not want a vehicle that feels underpowered or merely adequate.
   - I enjoy having power in reserve.

4. **I care a lot about technology.**
   - High-tech features are a real draw, not a minor bonus.
   - That includes modern UX, software feel, autonomy features, and overall engineering sophistication.

5. **I dislike intrusive driver-assist behavior.**
   - I do not want steering correction or lane features that feel like the car is fighting me.
   - I’m fine with features that can be easily disabled or tuned down.

6. **I want spending discipline.**
   - My realistic buy lane is around **$60k**.
   - More expensive vehicles can still be used as comparisons, but should not dominate the decision.

## Important context / likely biases

These are real, but should be treated carefully rather than assumed to decide the outcome:

- I have a long positive history with **Toyota**.
- I loved my **2014 Highlander Platinum** and still use it as a comfort/reference point.

  - low seating position
